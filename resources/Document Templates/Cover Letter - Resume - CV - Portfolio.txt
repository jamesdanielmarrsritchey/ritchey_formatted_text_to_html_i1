#Appendix

##Glossary of Terms, Abbreviations, and Acronyms

#######Badge

	In education, a badge (also referred to as a digital badge) is a digital record of academic achievement, which denotes the completion of an educational program, course, or exercise by a student. Badges are designed to be shared, and may also support being embedded into other pages.

#######Career Portfolio

	A career portfolio is a portfolio constructed for showcasing one's abilities to employers.

#######Certificate

	In education, a certificate is a record of academic achievement, in the form of a document, which denotes the completion of an educational program, or course by a student.

#######Certification

	A certification is a record, typically in the form of a document, of being certified as competent in a particular act, or skillset. When an organization certifies a someone, it means they are vouching for that person's abilities. Certifications are usually awarded upon the completion of a test, or hands-on evaluation. However, some organizations will also provide a complete non-formal educational program to prepare students first. Some professions require certification, meaning the professional must hold certain certifications, by law, to perform that work. The term certificate is sometimes used interchangeably with certification, but certificates do not expire, as they are record of academic accomplishemnt. A certification does expire, after a set amount of time.

#######C.E.U.

	A C.E.U. (Continuing Education Unit) is a measure of work applied to continuing education courses. It is similar to credit hours.

#######Continuing Education

	Continuing Education is non-credit (meaning it does not count towards a degree program) formal post-secondary education. The term is commonly used in Canada, and the United States. Continuing education is a form of professional development.

#######C.P.D.

	C.P.D. (Continuing Professional Development) is non-formal post-secondary education. The term is commonly used in the U.K.

#######Cover Letter

	A cover letter is an introductory letter, traditionally sent with a resume. In the past, when dropping off resumes in-person was the norm, cover letters were standard to include. Nowadays, a cover letter is typically only provided if it is explicitly requested.

#######Credit

	In education, credit (also called academic credit) is a measure of time (referred to as credit hours) applied to course work. In for-credit formal education programs, each course is assigned a certain number of credits, and the program as a whole will require the student to complete a certain number of credit hours.

#######C.V.

	In Canada, a C.V. (Curriculum Vitae) is a document which provides an indepth account of one's professional qualifications. However, in some countries, where the term resume is uncommon, a C.V. refers to a document which provides a concise selective account of one's professional qualifications. In Canada, most job applications expect a resume, but C.V.s are used in some very specific instances.

#######Degree

	In education, a degree is a record of academic achievement, in the form of a document, which denotes the completion of an educational program. Degree programs are longer than certificate programs.

#######Education

	Education is an intentional learning process. There are several types of education including: formal education, non-formal education, and informal education. Formal education is education which occurs in schools. On a resume, or a C.V. there is typically a section for listing education. It is typically titled "Education". This section is primarily used for listing formal education, but can be used for other types as well.

#######Employee

	An employee is person who works for a company, under contract. They are required to perform work as instructed.

#######Freelancer

	A freelancer is an independent contractor typically hired for projects that take little time to complete.

#######Further Education

	Further Education is non-credit (meaning it does not count towards a degree program) formal post-secondary education, and non-formal post-secondary education. The term further education is commonly used in the U.K.

#######Independent Contractor

	An independent contractor is a person who is self-employed, but does tasks for companies, or persons, under contract. They may have multiple clients at a time, and a free to perform work in the manner they see fit.

#######Intern

	In Canada, an intern is a non-employee member of a workplace, who is there to learn. They must still be paid at least minimum wage.

#######Licence

	In Canada, a licence is a legal grant of permission to perform certain acts. Some professions require licensure, meaning the employee must hold certain licences. The American spelling "license" is often used instead.

#######License

	In the United States of America, a license is a legal grant of permission to perform certain acts. Some professions require licensure, meaning the employee must hold certain licenses.

#######M.O.O.C.

	A M.O.O.C. (Massive Open Online Course) is an online course that is self-paced, and has no student limit. Generally M.O.O.C.s are non-formal education.

#######P.D.U.

	A P.D.U. (Professional Development Unit) is a measure of work applied to professional development courses. It is similar to credit hours.

#######Portfolio

	A portfolio is a collection of a person's completed works, awards, and relevant documents, used for assessment of their abilities. A career portfolio is a portfolio constructed for showcasing one's abilities to employers.

#######Professional Development

	Professional development is non-credit (meaning it does not count towards a degree program) formal post-secondary education, non-formal post-secondary education, and other activities undertaken to improve one's professional abilities (e.g., attending conferences). One of the most common forms of professional development is online non-formal education courses, offered as M.O.O.C.s.

#######Resume

	A resume is a document which provides a concise selective account of one's professional qualifications. It is generally 1-2 pages long, but can be longer for more well qualified persons.

#######Student Intern

	In Canada, a student intern is a non-employee member of a workplace, who is there to learn. Unless they are under the age of 18, they must be doing the internship as part of an educational program offered by a school. They are not required to be paid.

#######Volunteer

	A volunteer is someone who willingly labours for free at an organization, for the betterment of humanity.
